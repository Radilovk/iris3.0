IRIS PIPELINE PROMPTS v9 (FINAL)


================================================================================
IMAGE_FORMAT: UNWRAPPED_IRIS_STRIP (IMPORTANT — READ BEFORE ALL STEPS)
================================================================================

CRITICAL NOTICE:
The image provided to ALL pipeline steps is NOT a raw circular iris photograph.
It is an UNWRAPPED (unrolled/linearized) iris strip produced by a polar-to-rectangular
transformation of the circular iris. The circular ring of the iris has been "straightened"
into a horizontal rectangle.

STRIP LAYOUT (pixel dimensions):
- Full canvas: ~1280 × 390 px (includes labeled margins)
- Iris content region: ~1200 × 300 px (inside left/top margins)
- Left margin (~60 px): ring labels R0, R1, ..., R11 printed at each ring boundary
- Top margin (~50 px): minute tick marks labeled 0, 5, 10, 15, ..., 60 (every 5 min)
- Bottom area: eye side label ("RIGHT EYE" / "LEFT EYE"), "^ NASAL", "^ TEMPORAL"

COORDINATE MAPPING — HOW TO READ LOCATION IN THE RECTANGULAR IMAGE:

X AXIS (horizontal, left → right) = ANGULAR POSITION IN MINUTES:
  - Left edge of iris strip  → minute  0  (= 12 o'clock position, top of circle)
  - Quarter of strip width   → minute 15  (= 3 o'clock)
  - Half of strip width      → minute 30  (= 6 o'clock, bottom of circle)
  - Three-quarter of width   → minute 45  (= 9 o'clock)
  - Right edge of strip      → minute 60  (= 12 o'clock again, same as minute 0)
  Formula: minute = (x_pixel - left_margin) / strip_width * 60
  Direction: clockwise from 12 o'clock.
  Use the printed minute tick labels at the top of the image to read the minute directly.

Y AXIS (vertical, top → bottom) = RADIAL DEPTH IN RINGS:
  - Top of iris strip    → Ring 0  (innermost — adjacent to pupil edge)
  - Bottom of iris strip → Ring 11 (outermost — adjacent to iris/limbus edge)
  Formula: ring = (y_pixel - top_margin) / strip_height * 12
  Use the printed R0–R11 labels on the left margin to read the ring directly.

NASAL / TEMPORAL ORIENTATION (visible at bottom of image):
  RIGHT EYE: "^ TEMPORAL" ≈ minute 15 (3 o'clock), "^ NASAL" ≈ minute 45 (9 o'clock)
  LEFT  EYE: "^ NASAL"    ≈ minute 15 (3 o'clock), "^ TEMPORAL" ≈ minute 45 (9 o'clock)

RING GROUP ZONES (reference):
  R0        → IPB  (iris pupillary border)
  R1        → STOM (stomach ring)
  R2–R3     → ANW  (autonomic nerve wreath / collarette)
  R4–R9     → ORG  (organ zone — main iridology area)
  R10       → LYM  (lymphatic zone)
  R11       → SCU  (scurf rim / skin zone)

WHITE/BLANK BANDS in the strip = areas masked by eyelids (pre-processed before unrolling).
These correspond to the upper and lower eyelid occlusion zones in the original circular image.
The upper eyelid typically masks a horizontal band appearing at both the left edge (minutes 0–10)
and the right edge (minutes 50–60) of the strip — these two edges represent the same angular
region (12 o'clock), because the strip starts and ends at 12 o'clock.
The lower eyelid typically masks a band near minutes 25–35 (around 6 o'clock).

CRITICAL — DO NOT:
  - Attempt to detect a limbus ellipse or pupil circle in this image.
  - Apply atan2 or any circular-geometry formula to locate findings.
  - Compute radial distances from a center point.
  Instead: READ minute from X position using top tick labels, READ ring from Y position
  using left R-labels. This is the ONLY correct localization method for unwrapped strips.

ROLE: iris_geo_calibrator_v9
MODE: image_parse_only
INPUT: unwrapped_iris_strip   # rectangular image — see IMAGE_FORMAT section above
SIDE: {{side}}   # "R" or "L"

GOAL:
- Decide if the unwrapped strip is usable for iris analysis.
- Confirm v9 coordinate grid as printed on the image:
  - minutes 0..59: left-edge=0 (12 o'clock), quarter=15 (3 o'clock),
    half=30 (6 o'clock), three-quarter=45 (9 o'clock) — clockwise
  - 12 rings 0..11: top=R0 (inner/pupil side), bottom=R11 (outer/limbus side)

IGNORE:
White/blank bands (eyelid-masked regions) | glare/specular patches | areas outside the iris strip margins

COORDINATE READING (CRITICAL — UNWRAPPED STRIP):
- DO NOT detect limbus ellipse, pupil circle, or apply any circular geometry.
- DO NOT use atan2 or radial distance formulas.
- Minute (angular position): read directly from the X position of a feature using the
  minute tick labels printed at the top of the image.
  - Each tick interval = 5 minutes; interpolate for positions between ticks.
- Ring (radial depth): read directly from the Y position using the R0–R11 labels
  printed in the left margin.
  - Each row between two ring lines = one ring; the top row is R0.
- Reference for quarter-positions:
  - ~1/4 of strip width from left ≈ minute 15 (3 o'clock)
  - ~1/2 of strip width ≈ minute 30 (6 o'clock)
  - ~3/4 of strip width ≈ minute 45 (9 o'clock)

SPECULAR / GLARE HANDLING:
- Detect bright white or washed-out patches in the iris strip that lack texture.
- Locate their minute and ring range by reading the grid axes.
- Mark as invalidRegions with their minuteRange/ringRange.

EYELID OCCLUSION (UPPER / LOWER):
- Upper eyelid masking appears as a continuous white horizontal band near
  minutes 0–10 (left edge of strip) and/or minutes 50–60 (right edge of strip).
  Both edges represent the same 12 o'clock angular region of the original iris.
- Lower eyelid masking appears as a white band near minutes 25–35 (around 6 o'clock).
- If the white band at 12 o'clock (left and/or right strip edges) covers more than
  ~1/3 of the strip height in that minute range: usableUpperIris=false.

QUALITY GATE:
Set ok=false if any:
- focus="poor" (iris texture is blurry or unrecognizable throughout the strip)
- >35% of the strip area is white/blank (eyelid-masked or glare)
- Specular/glare covers the majority of the ANW zone (rings 2–3) in the strip
- The strip appears to be an invalid image (all white, all black, or not an iris strip)

refRay15Usable:
- In the unwrapped strip this means: is the minute-15 column (3 o'clock position —
  temporal for RIGHT eye, nasal for LEFT eye) visible and free of white masking or glare?
  Set to false if that vertical column is predominantly white/blank.

- atrophic_area
- collarette_defect_lesion
- radial_furrow
- deep_radial_cleft
- transversal_fiber
- structural_asymmetry

DEFINITIONS (strict):
- lacuna: oval/leaf gap; breaks fiber flow; not pure black; shallow->mid.
- crypt: small deep dark rhomboid/triangular hole; sharp edges; fibers terminate abruptly.
- giant_lacuna: very large lacuna dominating sector (>= ~8 minutes).
- atrophic_area: locally absent/flattened fiber texture (NOT glare).
- collarette_defect_lesion: notch/break/strong indentation exactly on ANW (rings 2–3).
- radial_furrow: narrow dark radial track from ANW outward overriding fibers.
- deep_radial_cleft: wider/deeper radial channel than radial_furrow.
- transversal_fiber: clear non-radial crossing line(s) cutting across radial fibers.
- structural_asymmetry: strong structural difference between two sectors not explained by glare.

STEP2C_consistency_validator
================================================================================

ROLE: iris_consistency_validator_v9
MODE: strict_json_only
INPUTS:

- PIG={{step2b_json}}

PREREQ:
- If GEO.ok != true: return error JSON.
- If STRUCT.error or PIG.error exists: return error JSON.

PURPOSE:
- Remove contradictions, duplicates, and range errors.
- Normalize ranges (no wrap), clamp values.
- Do NOT add new findings. Only keep/merge/split/drop.

LIMITS:
- maxFindingsOut = 60
- maxSplitsPerFinding = 2
- If >60 after cleanup: keep highest confidence first.

CONTRADICTION RULES:
1) scurf_rim vs sodium_ring:
   - If minute overlap >50% AND ring overlap >50%:
     - keep only one:
       - keep sodium_ring if notes imply light/milky/obscures texture
       - keep scurf_rim if notes imply dark rim and texture visible
     - if undecidable: drop both unless one confidence >= 0.80.
2) pigment_spot vs lacuna/crypt:
   - If pigment_spot overlaps structural gap: keep gap; drop pigment_spot.
3) lymphatic_rosary vs brushfield_like_spots:
   - If same area: keep rosary only if clearly chain/arc of nodules; else keep brushfield.
4) specular contamination:
   - Drop if overlaps GEO.invalidRegions >25%.

DEDUP / MERGE:
- Same type & strong overlap (minute>60% and ring>60%):
  - merge ranges by union (after normalization)
  - confidence = max
  - notes = shorter

RANGE NORMALIZATION:
- clamp minutes 0..59, rings 0..11.
- minuteRange must NEVER wrap:
  - if start>end => split into [start,59] + [0,end]
- ringRange must be start<=end; if start>end swap.
- point-like: if minute width==0 expand to ±1 unless confidence>=0.85 and focus good.
- very wide: if minute width>20 OR ring width>3 => confidence -=0.15; drop if <0.55.

GLOBAL CONSISTENCY:
- collarette_clean.ringRange must be [2,3] (clamp and reduce confidence by 0.20 if inconsistent).
- If GEO.usableUpperIris=false and a finding centered in minutes [57..59] or [0..3]:
  - confidence -= 0.10 (additional safety).
